Built because
nothing else worked.

I am building a game in UE5, using Rigify to animate it. When I started bringing animations into the engine, every animation started breaking in ways Blender never showed me clearly: spine tearing, hierarchy issues, export-only artifacts. I've always used Rigify so I was kinda stubborn about using a custom rig pipeline and creating the game rig from scratch and importing it.

I tried some tools that already existed but I still ended up with issues. They usually help create the game rig but none of them helped me actually debug what was happening wrong.

I got frustrated to the point where I started creating the addon myself. I never planned on releasing it but it became a much larger tool than I was planning on making. I think maybe it has a use for someone in the same situation as me. Or if they just want a faster workflow where you effectively remove the whole hand craft a rig, then a game rig, then transfer each animation — the tool helps with everything. And if you ever stumble upon an issue you've got a very strong debugging tool that will pinpoint what is wrong.

01 The problem

Most export tools stop at
"FBX created."

They do not tell you why the spine is wrong, why the hierarchy broke, or why the mesh deforms differently in-engine. You usually find that out after export, when debugging is slower and harder.

Rigify is excellent inside Blender. The problem is the gap between how Blender evaluates a rig and how a game engine evaluates a skeleton after export. That gap is where the failures live.

RigBridge was built for that gap.

Symptom 01

The skeleton looks wrong in-engine

A hierarchy issue can stay invisible in Blender and only show up after export. By the time you see it, you are already debugging in the wrong place.

Symptom 02

Animations that looked fine start tearing

The spine looks stable in Blender. In-engine it starts stuttering, tearing through the torso, or behaving inconsistently from action to action.

Symptom 03

The mesh deforms in ways you never painted

Weights that looked fine in Blender can break once the export skeleton changes, influences are dropped, or the engine renormalizes them differently on import.

02 How it works

What RigBridge does.

Build a clean export rig, bind the mesh, bake the animations, run checks, then export. Each step is built to tell you what failed when the result is wrong.

I
Generate Rig
Clean DEF-bone export rig from your Rigify setup.
Duplicates your Rigify rig keeping only DEF bones. Reparents each to its nearest DEF ancestor. Self-parent guard prevents broken hierarchies. Weighted bone protection keeps any bone with mesh weights regardless of filters. WGT widgets unlinked from outliner.
II
Bind Mesh
Armature modifier redirected at rest pose. No deflated mesh.
Switches the mesh modifier from Rigify to the export rig at rest pose, so Blender records correct inverse bind matrices. Secondary mesh modes: preserve weights, rigid-lock to nearest bone for props, or ignore.
III
Bake
Visual keying every frame. Engine-aware fixes applied post-bake.
Bakes all actions or a single selected one. Distributed spine scale applied post-bake for UE5/UE4 — Unreal-specific, Unity and Godot are not affected. Quaternion flip correction walks every fcurve, catching the 360° spin artifacts that commonly appear on arms and feet in-engine.
IV
Diagnose
23 operators. Know exactly what is broken before export.
Lives in its own dedicated Debug tab — separate from your main workflow. Quick Checks for common validations. Deep Analysis organised by Spine/Torso, Animation, Weights, and Bone Structure. Each operator tells you exactly what it found and where.
V
Export FBX
Per-engine settings. Batch export. Custom profile for any pipeline.
Built-in profiles for UE5, UE4, Unity, and Godot. Custom profile lets you configure any FBX pipeline manually. Batch export processes all actions with filename sanitization. Debug export mode for isolating problems.

What usually goes wrong

Export failures

Rigify and game-engine export are not the same system. The common failures are not dramatic until you are already in-engine: spine instability, broken parent chains, export-only rotation artifacts, mesh deformation that no longer matches what you saw in Blender. The hard part is not only fixing them. It is knowing where they entered the pipeline.
Shoulder jitter
A persistent per-frame vibration on the shoulders in UE5. Invisible in Blender. The bone looked correct at rest because root and chest are at the same position when nothing is moving — the problem only appeared in animation when the chest moved independently. Root cause: a silent self-parent assignment during hierarchy rebuild.
Spine micro-shake
The torso oscillated slightly on every frame. Misdiagnosed for a long time as a weight issue or UE5 import setting. Actual cause: Rigify bakes a 0.926 scale factor into DEF-spine.001 and DEF-spine.002. Blender hides it via inherit_scale=NONE. UE5 ignores that flag and propagates the scale through the whole chain. Confirmed by re-importing the FBX into Blender and measuring the scale directly.
Liquid mesh deformation
Face, chest, and some body areas deforming as if made of liquid. Vertices pulling in wrong directions, mesh collapsing and stretching unpredictably. Cause: keyword filters were silently stripping bones that had real mesh weights. The vertices weighted to those bones had nothing to follow and collapsed to world origin.
What RigBridge was built to handle

Traced and addressed

RigBridge was built around traced failures, not ideal cases. The fixes came from finding actual root causes rather than patching symptoms.
Shoulder jitter — root cause found
DEF-shoulder.L was being assigned as its own parent. Blender silently reparented it to root. One guard — if the DEF equivalent is the bone itself, skip. Shoulder jitter eliminated.
Spine shake — 14 attempts, one fix (UE5/UE4 only)
Rigify bakes 0.926 scale into DEF-spine bones. Concentrated on two bones it's visible. Distributed as 0.926^(1/n) across all DEF-spine bones — ~1% per bone, invisible. Accumulated product is 0.926 exactly. UE5 evaluates identically to Blender.
Liquid mesh — weighted bone protection
Face, breast, and palm bones were being stripped by keyword filters despite having real mesh weights. Vertices collapsed to world origin. Fix: scan all meshes first, build a protected set. No weighted bone can be removed regardless of filter.
360° spin — quaternion flip correction
Visual keying stores adjacent frames as q and −q (same rotation, opposite sign). UE5 interpolates linearly between them — 360° spin. Post-bake walk of every fcurve negates frames where dot product is negative.

04 Diagnostics

The exporter is only
half of it.

RigBridge was built on the assumption that the pipeline might fail. When it does, you need to know why. That is why the diagnostics are a core part of the addon, not an afterthought.

Most export tools automate the pipeline. RigBridge was built around the assumption that it might not work, and that when it fails, you need to know exactly where.

Hierarchy failures
Self-parent bugs, broken bone chains, wrong inherit_scale — invisible in Blender, broken in UE5. → Validate Rig, Critical Bone Chain Inspector, Skeleton Compat
Weight problems
Stripped weighted bones, rogue islands, micro-weights, missing influences — the mesh collapses or deforms wrong. → Weight Audit, Weighted but Missing, Selected Vert Inspector
Bake artifacts
Export-only pops, quaternion flips, frame coverage gaps — animations that look fine in Blender and break in-engine. → Bone Pop Detector, Deep Bake Verification, Verify Bake
Spine & scale
The 0.926 Rigify scale factor, bendy-bone constraint mismatch, per-frame oscillation on the torso and chest. → Spine Bake Analyser, Stage Tracer, Torso / Shoulder Pop Detector
Export failures
Root bone missing or misplaced, leftover constraints, wrong modifier stack, negative scale on mesh or armature. → Export Readiness, Pre-Bind Safety Check, NLA Pre-Bake Audit
Animation artifacts
360° spin on arms or feet, shoulder jitter, mesh deformation that doesn't match Blender on any frame. → Animation Mismatch, Stage Tracer, Space Solver Shootout
23 diagnostic and validation tools across Quick Checks and Deep Analysis

Quick Checks

Validate Rig
armature modifier target, duplicate bone names, non-deform bones remaining, deform bone weight coverage, object scale.
Weight Audit
unweighted vertices, over-influenced, micro-weights, rogue islands. selects problem verts in viewport. auto-fix available.
Verify Bake
counts bones with actual fcurves. detects if the control rig is the only animated rig (bake failed). shows frame count and bone coverage.
Skeleton Compatibility
compares rigify rig and game rig bone structure — missing bones, extra bones, parent mismatches, rest-pose position differences.
Export Readiness
meshes bound, no negative scale, root bone at origin, influence count within limits, no leftover constraints.
Shape Key Export Checker
armature modifier position, shape key values at export time, basis key order, and naming for engine compatibility.
Root Motion Validator
identifies moving root (root motion) vs static root (in-place). checks root bone position and keyframe coverage.
All seven run in one click.
No need to run them individually — Run All Checks handles the full quick check suite at once.

Deep Analysis

Stage Tracer
forensic stage-by-stage tracer. measures world position error at source → post-bake → post-correction. reports first bad stage.
Spine Bake Analyser
per-frame analysis of all DEF-spine bones. distinguishes consistent offset (bendy-bone issue) from per-frame jitter (constraint issue).
Torso / Shoulder Pop Detector
compares source vs export rig. only flags pops that exist in the export rig but not the source — isolates export-side artifacts.
Critical Bone Chain Inspector
compares source and export rig on parent chain, inherit_scale, rest position, and baked keys for bones most likely causing torso jitter.
Animation Mismatch
compares source rigify rig vs baked export rig on every frame. finds bones that move differently after export.
Bone Pop Detector
detects sudden frame-to-frame jumps in the baked export rig. finds shoulder and root popping that appears in UE5 but not Blender.
Deep Bake Verification
multi-frame bake verification across all actions. finds bake failures that a single-frame check misses.
NLA Pre-Bake Audit
rigify rig NLA state before baking — muted tracks, solo mode, overlapping strips, stacked active actions.
Selected Vert Inspector
select vertices in the jittering area and run this. reports which bones drive those verts, whether baked, whether twist bones.
Weighted but Missing
finds bones that influence the mesh but are missing from the export rig, have no baked keys, or are effectively static.
Pre-Bind Safety Check
validates the export rig before mesh bind — confirms all weighted DEF bones exist and critical parent chains are intact.
Bone Scale Validator
bones with animated scale, non-unit scale, or negative scale. unexpected scale keys produce deformation artifacts in UE5.
Twist / Helper Bone Audit
detects twist and helper bones influencing the mesh. missing or unanimated twist bones cause shoulder and wrist deformation in UE5.
Critical Bone Checklist
validates that root, pelvis, spine, and hip bones exist, are weighted, and have baked keyframe data.
Space Solver Shootout
tests every local-space conversion method on a bone and ranks by reconstruction accuracy. tells you which math is correct for your rig.
Debug Export
exports the active action only with strict no-simplification settings — step=1, simplify=0, deform bones only. for isolating export problems.
