What it is

A world that does not
scale to you.

A session-based action RPG built around a simple rule: enemies are fixed. The difficulty never adjusts. You do not get stronger by grinding — you get stronger by learning. Routes, patterns, risks, consequences. The world becomes readable over time because you built a mental map of it.

Every run is a three-day arc. You win by killing the Day 3 boss. You lose when your lives reach zero. There is no extraction.

The structure

Three days.
Two maps.
One boss.

Day 1 takes place on one map. Day 2 moves you to a different map — and the consequences of Day 1 start arriving. Day 3 reuses Day 2's map.

That reuse is intentional. You spent Day 2 learning that terrain — every route, every shortcut. Day 3 puts a boss into that same world and tells it to hunt you. The map you know becomes the map you share with something that wants you dead.

Day 1 feels free because the player believes it is — not because the game is lenient. Consequences exist on Day 1. Many of them are delayed. They surface on Day 2.

Combat

No classes.
No ability trees.

Your weapons define your moveset, your skills, your identity. Dual-wield sword and axe and your combo chains are different from dual swords. Each weapon hand has three independent skill slots — Q, E, R — with their own cooldowns and behaviors. Cycling your active hand mid-combat swaps the skill bar.

Damage is multi-channel. A fire sword splits its base damage across Slash and Fire simultaneously, each mitigated independently by the target's resistances. 18 damage types across 5 categories. Status buildup on every hit.

The GDD originally had universal parry, weapon weight tiers, and six critical types. All three were redesigned during implementation. Parry is now opt-in via weapon skill subclass only. Dodge type is driven by chest armor mobility class, not weapon weight. Two crit types remain.

The world remembers

Every action
accumulates.

Factions track violence, restraint, repeated aggression, and betrayals. Individual NPCs remember if you attacked them, completed their quest, or spent gold with them. Vendors adjust pricing. Questlines open, branch, or collapse permanently. There is no clean slate inside a run.

The implementation behind this is a single GameplayTagContainer on the RunStateComponent. Every faction, NPC, vendor, and quest reads from the same source and reacts. Completing a quest fires a tag. The vendor two maps away can see that tag.

Day Three

It knows
you are alive.

A unique boss — drawn from a pool that exists only for Day 3 — spawns at a handpicked location on the map. It has two phases. Presence: it moves toward you slowly, reshaping which routes are viable, while you loot and prepare. Hunt: it locks on and chases. Cannot be outrun.

You can fight it at minute 5 if you are ready. You can wait until minute 22 and use every remaining POI. The timer is yours to manage — until it is not.

The run ends only by death or killing the boss. There is no other exit. Saving mid-session is an exit valve, not an extraction — reloading never changes anything about the world state. The seed is locked.

What changed during implementation

The GDD was written before a line of C++ existed.Decisions

Several systems were redesigned once implementation revealed the original design did not hold up. These are not mistakes — they are how the game got better.

Parry System Phase 1
Original GDD
Universal parry window on block input. All weapons could parry by default. CanParry flag on the weapon mechanics bitmask.
Current Design
Parry removed entirely as a universal mechanic. Now opt-in only — specific weapons can have a parry via weapon skill subclass. No weapon parries by default.
Dodge System Phase 8
Original GDD
Weapon weight tiers (Ultralight through Colossal) determined dodge type. Heavier weapons meant slower, shorter dodges.
Current Design
EWeaponWeight removed entirely. Dodge type — Dash, Dodge, or Roll — is driven by chest armor mobility class. Light armor = Dash. Heavy armor = Roll.
Critical Hits Phase 1
Original GDD
Six critical types: Backstab, Riposte, StanceBreak, Sleep, Dismount, Execution. Each with its own conditions and behavior.
Current Design
Collapsed to two: Execution (conditional, triggered by stance break or low HP) and ChanceBased (probability-driven). Everything else removed.
Map Architecture Phase 9
Original GDD
Map A covered Days 1 and 2. Map B was used only on Day 3. Two maps for a three-day arc.
Current Design
Map A = Day 1 only. Map B = Days 2 and 3. Day 3 reuses Day 2's map deliberately — the familiar terrain becomes the hunting ground.
Weapon Skills Phase 4
Original GDD
One "weapon art" per weapon. Single skill, no cooldown system, no charges.
Current Design
Three skill slots per hand (Q/E/R). Each slot has its own cooldown, charge count, and UWeaponSkillBehavior subclass. Slot1 and Slot2 are fully independent. Hand cycling via X swaps the active skill bar.
Inventory Phase 7
Original GDD
Weight-based inventory system. Broadly described without a grid or slot structure.
Current Design
8×5 2D grid with variable item sizes. Small items take 1×1. Larger items take more. Blessing-based grid expansion. Items physically fill space — RE4-style.
Where it is and where it goes

Phases 1–13 complete. Phase 14 is the blocker.The Plan

The C++ architecture is built. 34,767 lines across 60 files. Two full code reviews. Every identified bug resolved. The systems are wired end-to-end — damage, status buildup, run state, world reactivity, inventory, equipment, weapon skills. What does not exist yet is anything you can see on screen.

Animation Pipeline
Getting Rigify characters into UE5 was not straightforward.
Spine scale propagation invisible in Blender, breaking in UE5 on every animation. Shoulder jitter from a silent self-parent assignment during hierarchy rebuild. Liquid mesh deformation from weighted bones being stripped by keyword filters. Traced every failure to its root cause and built a tool to fix it. That tool — RigBridge — shipped as a separate product. The animation pipeline for REMEMBERED is now clean.
